# ROSA vs Traditional Platforms: Competitive Analysis

## Executive Summary

**ROSA** is a blockchain-based rotating savings circle protocol built on Anoma's intent-centric architecture. Unlike PayPal Pools, Cash App, and Venmo—which commercialize centuries-old community savings practices while capturing value and imposing platform risk—ROSA returns sovereignty, privacy, and zero-cost coordination to the communities that invented these practices.

The informal ROSCA (Rotating Savings and Credit Association) market moves **$500B+ annually** globally, with **1+ billion people** participating in trust-based savings circles. Yet this market is increasingly captured by Silicon Valley payment platforms that charge fees, hold user data, freeze accounts without explanation, and profit from community wealth.

ROSA disrupts this extraction model.

## Part 1: The Platforms

### PayPal Pools

**Launched:** November 2024 (relaunch; original Money Pools existed ~2014-2018)

**How It Works:**
- Create a pool in the PayPal app
- Share pool link via text, email, WhatsApp
- Friends contribute (PayPal account required to **create** a pool; optional for contributors)
- Organizer transfers collected funds to PayPal balance or bank account
- Money can be spent via PayPal balance or withdrawn

**Fee Structure:**
- **Free** to create and manage pools
- **Fees apply when:**
  - Converting currency (standard FX fees)
  - Sending money internationally
  - Example: 2.9% + $0.30 on P2P transfers in some jurisdictions
  
**Privacy Model:**
- PayPal sees all transaction metadata
- Pool visibility: Participants can see who contributed what
- Account email & verification data tied to PayPal identity
- Pools are linked to PayPal accounts for AML/KYC compliance
- No anonymity; full transaction surveillance

**Geographic Availability:**
- Launched in UK, US, Germany, Italy, Spain (as of Q4 2024)
- Expanding but limited vs. other platforms

**Revenue Model:**
- Direct transaction fees on currency conversion
- Indirect: Holds funds in PayPal accounts (earns float interest)
- User data & behavioral analytics

**Risk:**
- **Account Freezing**: PayPal can freeze accounts for up to **180 days** without clear explanation
- **Documented cases**: Users report $15,000+ frozen funds during "random reviews"
- **No legal recourse**: Terms of Service allow unilateral action; disputes require mailing to Nebraska address
- **For Pools**: Collecting pool funds could trigger account holds for "suspicious activity"

### Cash App Pools

**Launched:** July 2025 (most recent of the three)

**How It Works:**
- Organizer creates a pool with funding goal
- Organizer sets $Cashtag invite OR generates shareable link
- Non-Cash App users contribute via Apple Pay or Google Pay (no signup required)
- Organizer sees ledger of all contributions
- Money transfers to organizer's Cash App account or bank

**Fee Structure:**
- **Free** to create and manage pools
- **Fees apply on:**
  - Transfers to bank accounts (varies by bank)
  - Card-dependent fees (debit vs. credit card processing)
  - No explicit per-contribution fee
  
**Privacy Model:**
- Block (Cash App parent) sees transaction metadata
- Pool organizer has full visibility of contributor names/amounts
- Linked to Block identity for AML/KYC
- No privacy for contributors; publicly visible to organizer

**Geographic Availability:**
- US-focused (typical for Cash App/Block)
- Broader than PayPal Pools, narrower than Venmo

**Revenue Model:**
- Transaction fees on withdrawals
- Indirect: Float on held funds
- User data monetization (Block's business model)

**Advantage over PayPal:**
- Doesn't require participants to have Cash App accounts
- Accepts Apple Pay / Google Pay (lower friction)
- Newer, fewer documented freezing incidents so far

### Venmo Groups

**Launched:** November 2023

**How It Works:**
- Create a group within Venmo
- Add members (must have Venmo)
- Add expenses to group ledger
- App auto-calculates who owes whom
- Members settle via Venmo payments
- **Explicitly NOT a pooling feature**—it's expense splitting/tracking

**Fee Structure:**
- **Free** to use Venmo Groups
- Fees apply only if transferring via credit card (3% + $0.25)
- Debit card transfers are free

**Privacy Model:**
- Venmo's default: Public transaction feed (users can make private)
- Group members see all transaction details
- All payment history visible to PayPal (Venmo's parent)
- AML/KYC compliance requires identity verification

**Geographic Availability:**
- Broader than PayPal Pools or Cash App
- US and some international markets

**Revenue Model:**
- Credit card transaction fees
- Indirect: Float on held balances
- User engagement & data analytics

**Key Limitation:**
- Not a true pooling system—no collective pot accumulation
- Better for splitting a single dinner than coordinating rotating savings
- Doesn't support pure ROSCA mechanics (rotation, waiting for turn)

## Part 2: The Cultural Origins They Commercialized

### What Are ROSCAs?

A **Rotating Savings and Credit Association** is a trust-based financial arrangement where:
- A group (typically 10-20 people) agrees on a fixed contribution
- All members contribute to a pool each period (weekly, monthly)
- One member receives the **entire pot** each period
- **Members rotate** until everyone has received their full amount
- **No interest charged.** You get back exactly what you contributed.

ROSCAs are ancient, spanning 500+ years and existing across every continent. They predate modern banking by centuries.

---

### Susu (West Africa)

**Origin:** Nigeria/Ghana (Yoruba tradition)
**Etymology:** "Susu" comes from Yoruba meaning "to save money little by little"
**Spread:** To Caribbean (Partner, Sou-Sou), Canada, US diaspora

**Traditional Model:**
- Daily or weekly collection by a trusted "Susu Collector"
- Collector visits market vendors, workers, small business owners
- Gathers small daily amounts (e.g., 2-5% of daily earnings)
- Returns monthly savings to each saver
- Collector takes 1 day's collection as fee

**Why It Worked:**
- Forced savings discipline (can't skip; collector visits daily)
- Social accountability (community knows your contribution)
- Access to lump sums impossible through individual saving
- No credit score needed
- **For women especially**: Path to capital for small business, housing, education

**Statistics:**
- ~3/4 of Caribbean immigrants in NYC (1980s) participated in Susus
- Still primary savings mechanism in Ghana, Nigeria, diaspora communities
- Estimated 1+ billion people globally use some form of Susu

**Evidence of Commercialization:**
- PayPal Pools was designed after researching Susu practices
- Targets exact same demographic (first-generation immigrants, underbanked)
- Recreates convenience of Susu but captures the value

---

### Tanda (Latin America)

**Origin:** Mexico, Central America, distributed throughout Latin diaspora
**Also Known:** "San" in Dominican Republic, "Sol" in El Salvador
**Etymology:** Spanish "turn" or "round"

**Traditional Model:**
- 5-20 members form a circle
- Monthly or biweekly contributions
- Lottery or auction determines who receives that month's pot
- Often tied to cultural celebrations, weddings, religious events

**Cultural Significance:**
- Deeply embedded in Catholic financial traditions (interest-free as per religious teaching)
- Women-led, especially for wedding/household savings
- Extended family participation (multi-generational)
- Community trust mechanism in regions with low banking penetration

**Scale:**
- Informal estimates: 50+ million Latinos participate in Tandas
- Estimated $50B+ in annual Tanda flows in Latin America & diaspora

---

### Chit Funds (South Asia)

**Origin:** Southern India (Tamil Nadu, Kerala)
**Timeline:** 1000+ years old (documented in historical records)
**Etymology:** From Sanskrit/Tamil roots meaning "note" or "contract"
**Also Known:** In different regions: Nidhi, Kutti-Chittu, Bishi

**Traditional Model:**
- 5-50 members (often caste, profession, or family-based)
- Fixed monthly contributions
- **Auction model**: Members bid for early access (premium = how much they're willing to forfeit)
- First month: Highest bidder receives full pot minus their bid amount
- Later months: Lower bids (less need = less bidding)

**Cultural/Religious Significance:**
- Halal & Shariah-compliant (no interest, no riba)
- Compatible with Hindu/Buddhist practice (no usury)
- Formalized into semi-legal structures with written bylaws

**Scale:**
- Estimated 100+ million South Asians participate
- Chit funds handle estimated $20-50B annually
- Some organized as official "Chit Fund Companies" with light regulation

**Why It Matters:**
- Chit funds have existed **uninterrupted for 1000+ years**
- Predates every digital payments platform by a millennium
- Proven working model for financial inclusion

---

### Hagbad / Ayuuto (Somalia)

**Origin:** Somalia
**Etymology:** "Ayuuto" = Somali for "help"; roots in Italian "aiuto" (historical influence)
**Also Known:** "Hagbad" (traditional name)

**Traditional Model:**
- Women-led (primarily)
- 10-20 participants, often multi-generational
- Weekly or monthly contributions
- Rotating distribution
- Interest-free
- **Tied to community mutual aid**: Food security, education, dowry, business capital

**Historical Context:**
- Developed in context of limited state banking infrastructure
- During civil conflict, Ayuuto became only reliable savings mechanism
- Survived decades of economic collapse, diaspora displacement
- Core to Somali-American community cohesion

**Significance:**
- **Documented by Al Jazeera, UN, NGOs** as critical survival mechanism during instability
- Women hold power in Ayuuto structures (rare in formal finance)
- **Cross-border function**: Connects Somalia diaspora (US, Canada, UK, Middle East) to home communities

**Scale:**
- Conservative estimate: 10+ million Somalis globally participate
- Flows estimated at $1-5B annually (informal, hard to measure)

---

### Stokvel (South Africa)

**Origin:** South Africa
**Etymology:** "Stokvel" comes from English "stock fairs"—rotating cattle auctions of English settlers (1800s)
**Timeline:** 150+ years in modern form; older practices predate European arrival
**Also Known:** "Imbizo" (Zulu), "Umgalelo" (Sotho), "Tinhlelo" (Xhosa)

**Traditional Model:**
- Legally recognized rotating credit unions
- 12+ members
- Fixed weekly/monthly contributions
- Rotating payouts OR group investment/purchase

**Types of Stokvels:**
- **Contribution Stokvels**: Pure rotating savings
- **Investment Stokvels**: Pool money for stock market/real estate
- **Grocery Stokvels**: Collect supermarket coupons, distribute
- **Burial Stokvels**: Insurance model for funeral costs
- **Party Stokvels**: Social events, members share profits

**Scale & Regulation:**
- **800,000 active stokvels in South Africa**
- **50% of adult South Africans** participate in at least one stokvel
- **R50 billion (~$2.7B USD) annual investment** in stokvels
- **Regulated by NASASA** (National Stokvel Association of South Africa)—official self-regulatory organization since 1988
- NASASA is registered Financial Co-operative with Prudential Authority oversight

**Why Stokvels Matter:**
- Only rotating savings system with **formal regulatory recognition**
- Largest documented ROSCA economy by annual flow
- Proof that ROSCAs can scale to millions of participants
- Model: Community-self-regulation + light government oversight

### Why Traditional Finance Failed to Capture This

**Key Insight:** ROSCAs work **because** they're not formal banking.
- Banks require credit scores (most ROSCA participants don't have/need them)
- Banks charge fees (ROSCAs charge none)
- Banks are impersonal (ROSCAs are community-based)
- Banks demand documentation (ROSCAs demand only trust)
- Banks take deposits and lend them out (ROSCAs only redistribute)

**Result:** 1+ billion people have been **excluded from formal finance** but are **financially sophisticated** through ROSCAs. They don't trust banks because banks haven't earned it.

## Part 4: How PayPal/Venmo/Cash App Exploit This Market

### The Extraction Model

| Platform | What They Take | From Whom | How |
|----------|---------------|-----------|-----|
| **PayPal Pools** | 2.9% + $0.30 on transfers | Pool creators | Transaction fees + FX markup |
| **Cash App Pools** | ~1-3% on bank withdrawal | Organizers | Debit fees, spread |
| **Venmo Groups** | 3% + $0.25 on credit card payments | Users settling debts | Payment processing |
| **All Three** | **Interest on float** | All users | Hold funds for days/weeks, earn investment returns |
| **All Three** | **User data** | All users | Behavioral/transactional analytics, targeted ads |

### Account Freezing: The Hidden Tax

Both PayPal and Venmo can **freeze accounts for 180 days** with minimal explanation.

**Documented Cases:**
- **Venmo**: 103 out of 332 CFPB complaints (2019-2021) involved account freezes
- **PayPal**: Widespread Reddit/forum reports of $5,000-$30,000 frozen for "suspicious activity"
- **Excuse**: AML/KYC compliance, fraud detection
- **Reality**: Unilateral power to hold customer money indefinitely

**For Pools Specifically:**
- Large collective deposits could trigger "unusual activity" flags
- Organizer's account gets frozen → entire group's money inaccessible
- No recourse except filing CFPB complaint (takes 45+ days)

**Example:** A Susu group collects $10,000 from 10 people. PayPal freezes the organizer's account. The 10 contributors lose access to their cash indefinitely. Platform earns float interest on the frozen amount.

## Part 6: ROSA—The Alternative Model

### What ROSA Is

**ROSA** (Rotating Savings Agent) is a smart contract protocol built on **Anoma's intent-centric architecture** that enables true peer-to-peer rotating savings circles without platform intermediation.

### How ROSA Works

**Intent-Based Coordination:**
1. Members express intent: "I want to participate in a 12-person weekly $200 pool"
2. Intents are matched on a decentralized solver network
3. Smart contract enforces contributions & rotation automatically
4. No platform sees funds, no platform holds assets, no platform can freeze

**Mechanics:**
- Members fund sovereign self-custody wallets (or use stablecoins they control)
- Pool smart contract collects contributions at set times
- Rotation order is cryptographically determined or pre-agreed
- Each rotation, funds are transferred directly to recipient wallet
- Contract is immutable—no admin can freeze, reverse, or interfere

**Technical Stack:**
- Built on Anoma intents protocol
- Uses USDC or local stablecoins on any chain
- No platform custody of funds
- Decentralized settlement via solver network
- Optional privacy via Anoma's private sequencing

### Why ROSCAs Are Made for Blockchain

ROSCAs work because of **trust** in people, not institutions.

Traditional ROSCAs rely on:
- Social pressure (community knows if you don't pay)
- Reputation (cheaters get blacklisted)
- Informal enforcement (community action against defaults)
- High-touch personal relationships (in-person meetings)

Blockchain ROSCAs add:
- **Cryptographic enforcement** (code replaces social trust for settlement)
- **Transparency** (all transactions visible to members on-chain)
- **Immutability** (no admin can reverse, freeze, or change rules)
- **Sovereignty** (no intermediary, no platform risk)
- **Scalability** (add millions of members, still works)
- **Speed** (automatic rotation, no manual transfers)

**Result:** ROSCAs move from "impossible at scale without trusted intermediary" to "works at any scale with code-enforced rules."
